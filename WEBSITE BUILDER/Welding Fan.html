Welding Fan Project

Back to Projects

Introduction

The welding section at Sable Metal required improved ventilation. The existing fume hoods did not draw enough air, so I was tasked with installing a proper exhaust fan. I began by inspecting the current system and outlining the steps necessary for installation: calculate required CFM, select a suitable fan, design and fabricate a mount, and install the fan.

Research

I analyzed ventilation basics and focused on CFM (cubic feet per minute) ratings. Matching the exhaust fan to the intake system is critical; otherwise, it could overwork the existing fans. The intake fans were rated at 1700 CFM each, connected in parallel, so the exhaust fan needed to match ~1700 CFM to balance the airflow effectively.

Installation

I designed two “A”-shaped brackets to mount the fan to the ceiling railing, accounting for vibration. After securing the fan to the brackets, I connected it to the existing duct using duct tape to prevent leaks. Finally, the fan was wired to the same outlet as the intake fans, ensuring synchronized operation.

Results

After testing the system during welding operations, the airflow in the fume hood was noticeably stronger. The exhaust fan balanced perfectly with the intake system, improving safety and overall efficiency in the welding booth.
