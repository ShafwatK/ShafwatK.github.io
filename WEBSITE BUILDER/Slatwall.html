Introduction

The company had recently created its welding section, which was in desperate need of upgrades. One of the biggest issues was the lack of space to store tools and equipment — the area was disorganized and inefficient. I spoke with the welding team to understand their needs and quickly realized that vertical storage would be the most effective solution. This led to the design and installation of a custom slatwall and shelf system.

Planning Process

I first researched existing slatwall designs in the shop and online, aiming for something both cost-effective and easy to manufacture. Most were made of wood, which wasn’t suitable for the welding environment due to heat and sparks. Instead, I opted for a custom metal design that would be durable, heat-resistant, and flexible.

Unlike complex folded structures, I simplified the design to a flat metal sheet with precision-cut slots. This reduced costs while maintaining strength and ease of use. Using simulation software, I optimized slot spacing to balance strain across the wall and shelf. Below is the final SolidWorks model of my design.

Assembly

The assembly plan was simple: secure the slatwall with 1/4-20 bolts through each corner, extending to mount another slatwall on the opposite side. Execution, however, introduced challenges — drilling required perfect alignment, and some holes were misaligned. To fix this, I used a slightly larger drill bit for tolerance, reinforced with washers, and carefully bent bolts with a hammer for alignment. This maintained both strength and removability despite small errors in drilling.

Final Result

With the slatwall installed, I designed custom shelves and pegs tailored for each tool. This maximized vertical space usage, reduced clutter, and followed LEAN principles for workplace organization. The welding section became significantly more efficient and professional.
