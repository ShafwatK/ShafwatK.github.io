Introduction

One of the largest orders for Sable Metal involved producing a significant number of doors. These doors were fully assembled in the shop before shipping. The existing assembly process was slow and inefficient, so I was tasked with optimizing it and documenting steps to ensure repeatability.

Documentation

I closely observed and worked with the assembly team to understand their workflow. Notes and photos were taken for each step, and a detailed document was created outlining the full process, including time required. The SolidWorks drawing helped standardize the process. Through inspection and discussions, I identified key inefficiencies:

  • Hand-measuring every hole before drilling — slow and error-prone.
  • Hardware dimensions in the shop didn’t match CAD models, causing adjustments mid-process.

Modifications

To solve the drilling problem, I designed custom jigs that aligned with other door components. These jigs allowed precise match drilling, eliminating repeated manual measurements and speeding up the process.

Results

Implementing the modifications significantly improved the assembly process. Total assembly time decreased from 4.5 hours to 4 hours — roughly a 12% improvement. Standardized documentation ensured future teams could follow the optimized process without confusion.
